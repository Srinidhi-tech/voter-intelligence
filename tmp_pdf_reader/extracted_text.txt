Requirement-2 :How EVMs and VVPAT's work ,The 
Concept: 
EVM (Electronic Voting Machine): ◦A simple electronic device with candidate buttons
◦Has two units — Control Unit (with polling oﬃcer) and Balloting Unit (where you press the 
button)
◦Once you press a candidate's button, your vote is locked in
◦It is a standalone device — not connected to internet or any network, so it literally cannot be 
hacked remotely
•VVPAT (Voter Veriﬁable Paper Audit Trail):
◦A printer-like machine attached to EVM
◦After you press the button, a paper slip prints showing the candidate's name, symbol and 
party
◦You can see it through a glass window for 7 seconds
◦Then it auto-cuts and falls into a sealed box
◦This is proof that your vote was recorded correctly

Requirement-4: 
What Happens on Election Day — Step by Step 
The Problem: First-time voters are nervous and confused about what to actually do at the polling 
booth.
The Process: 
1.Check your polling booth address (on your voter ID or NVSP portal)
2.Carry your Voter ID or any of 12 approved ID proofs (Aadhaar, passport, PAN etc.)
3.Go to the booth in your designated time slot
4.Your name is checked in the electoral roll by the oﬃcer
5.Your ﬁnger gets ink marked (to prevent double voting)
6.You get a slip and go to the Balloting Unit
7.Press the button next to your chosen candidate
8.VVPAT shows the paper slip for 7 seconds
9.A beep conﬁrms your vote is recorded
10.You leave — done!

Requirement-5: 
Rights as a Voter 
The Problem: Most voters don't know they have legal rights at the polling booth.
Key rights people don't know about: 
•Right to secret ballot — nobody can force you to reveal who you voted for

•Right to not be denied voting — even if your name has a spelling error, oﬃcers must help you
•Right to assistance — disabled/elderly voters can bring a companion to help
•Right to complain — if someone oﬀers you money for vote, you can report it on cVIGIL app

Requirement-6: 
How Votes Are Counted and Results Declared 
The Problem: People watch news on result day but don't understand how the process actually 
works.
The Process: 
1.After polling ends, EVMs are sealed and stored in a strongroom with security
2.On counting day, EVMs are brought to the counting center
3.VVPAT slips from randomly selected booths are counted and matched with EVM data
4.Votes are counted round by round, each round covering a few booths
5.Running totals are announced after each round
6.Candidate with the highest votes wins (simple plurality — not majority needed)
7.The Returning Oﬃcer oﬃcially declares the winner
8.Results are published on the ECI website in real time .

Requirement-8: 
Model Code of Conduct (MCC) 
The Problem: People don't know what political parties and candidates are legally allowed to do 
during elections — so they can't identify violations.
What is MCC: 
•A set of guidelines issued by the Election Commission of India the moment election dates are 
announced
•It stays in force until results are declared
Key rules under MCC: 
•Government cannot announce new schemes or policies (to prevent vote buying with public 
money)
•No religious or caste-based campaigning
•Candidate cannot bribe voters with cash, gifts or liquor
•Political rallies need permission from local authorities
•24 hours before polling — all campaigning must stop (called campaign silence period)
•Government machinery (vehicles, buildings) cannot be used for campaigning
